## Wealth Management TLDR

*Audience: French-connected investors with UEMOA/Africa presence*

1. **Tax residency — the 183-day trap** (Oseille TV, 06-09): 183 days is a necessary but NOT sufficient condition for non-residency. French tax authorities assess center of economic interests (business), family ties (spouse, children), and habitual abode. For UEMOA-based investors, document ALL ties comprehensively and get a tax ruling (rescrit fiscal) before declaring departure.

2. **Exit France — plan before you leave** (Oseille TV, 06-08): Exit tax applies to unrealized gains on equity securities exceeding €800K. If using assurance-vie, PEA, or stock options: liquidate or restructure BEFORE officially registering domicile change. Consult a notaire specializing in expatriation 12+ months before departure date.

3. **Lifetime income tax avoidance** (Finary, 06-07): The most powerful structures for long-term tax optimization in France: (a) PEA — cap gains tax-free after 5 years, up to €150K deposit; (b) Assurance-vie — 30% flat tax drops to ~24% after 8 years + €4,600 annual abattement per person; (c) Holding + dividend vs. salary optimization if self-employed; (d) Geo-arbitrage to zero-tax jurisdiction (UAE, Qatar, Mauritius — all with UEMOA proximity).

4. **Private banking access for UEMOA clients** (Finary, 06-10): Tier 1 private banks (UBS, Pictet, Lombard Odier, Indosuez Wealth Management) serve francophone African clients. Minimum typically €750K–€1M. Key advantage: offshore SICAV structures with multi-currency allocation, estate planning, and discretionary management not available through retail channels. Indosuez specifically has a West Africa desk.

5. **Wealth concentration risk** (Finary, 06-09): Wealthy French families lose fortunes through: (a) single-asset concentration (real estate or unlisted shares); (b) succession disputes under forced heirship rules; (c) political seizure risk (nationalization, freezing). Mitigation: cap any single asset at <30% of net worth, use holding structures with succession planning, maintain >20% in liquid international assets.

6. **Geopolitical risk — diversify beyond EUR assets** (Oseille TV, 06-06 + 06-10): China/Iran geopolitical escalation + French surveillance state expansion → maintain meaningful USD/CHF allocation. For UEMOA investors, CFA franc peg to EUR provides some stability but adds French political exposure. Consider 10-15% USD-denominated assets as geopolitical hedge.
